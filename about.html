AI, behaviour, and the systems in between.

Abhishek Kumar Jha holds a Ph.D. in Management Information Systems from the Indian Institute of Management Indore, with a research focus at the intersection of artificial intelligence, natural language processing, information privacy, and behavioural science. His work blends computational techniques with psychological theory to explore how humans interact with intelligent systems in decision-making contexts.

Before joining SPJIMR, he was a lecturer at Deakin University, teaching postgraduate courses in machine learning, cybersecurity, and predictive analytics. He has completed advanced research training through GSERM at the University of St. Gallen and Participant-Focused Teaching Training by Harvard Business School. His honours include the Best Doctoral Student Award at IIM Indore, research grants from NASMEI, and multiple best-paper awards.

His current research examines the design, trust, and effectiveness of AI-driven systems across marketing, recommender systems, and privacy-enhancing technologies. He serves as a reviewer for journals including Marketing Intelligence & Planning, the Journal of Organisational Computing and Electronic Commerce, and the Journal of Business Research, and is a contributor to MISQ Insider.

Why this site exists

Most AI education either treats AI as a subject for data scientists alone, or stays at the level of breathless hype. This curriculum takes a third path: build the judgment to evaluate, adopt, govern, and study AI — paired always with a free tool and a small thing you can actually do. It is offered free, in public, for managers and researchers alike.
